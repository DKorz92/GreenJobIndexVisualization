Documentation

Choose a MSA

First, you have to choose a MSA of interest. You can click on the field in the map or search for the name in the field on the left and click on the it.
For this documentation we are going to check the situation for Boise City.

Analysis: What's the actual situation?

After selecting your city of interest, you can compare the MSA with his neighbors in the green index ranking. For the following analysis we add Evansville to our selection.

Another tool to investigate the situation of a MSA is the Over-Time-Panel. Here you can see the development of the green index of all selected MSAs.

Choose a set of occupations

The Occupation-Space-Panel gives you an overview over the specialiced green and non-green occupations, visualiced with an filled point.
It's clear to see, that Boise and Evansville have the quite same number of specialiced green occupation. Houston has a much better specialication, whereas Madera has a very low number of specialiced occupations.

To decide, which occupation you want to specialice in your MSA, the Occupation-Graph-Panel gives you two main criterias: First the direct increase of the green index and second the estimated easiness of specialication.
Next to this, the sallary of each occupation is shown by circle radius and color: The bigger (bluer) the circle, the higher the salary. Therefore you can choose between the salary in the MSA, the USA or the relative value (MSA/USA).
A grey-drawn circle means, that for this occupation are no data stored in the database.

For this consideration we select the two occupations "Solar Photovoltaic Installers" and "Wind Turbin Service Technicans", which are potentialy hard to specialice but have a high increase of the green index.

Choose a set of industries

Next we have to choose a set of industries, with wich we want to attracte workers in the selected occupations. The graphs show the percentage amount of workers of each occupation in the single industries.

Most of the industries just occupy one sort of workers, but if we invest in self-employed workers or the "Power and communication line ..."-industry we get both sorts. Which to choose?
A usefull tool could be the 10-year-projection.

Here we can see, that the self-employed workers will loose influence in our selected occupations, whereas the other industry will gain influence. So to invest in the last one could be sensefull.

Overview and create a new layer

Now we have a rough idea how to continue. The last panel concludes you the done selection and offers you the possibility to create a new "What-If-Scenario".
With a click on one of the selected MSAs the server calculates the minimal number of workers for each selected industry, which have to attracte, to specialice the selected occupations.

And now...?

A missing function is, that you can investigate now the MSA under the new circumstances.
